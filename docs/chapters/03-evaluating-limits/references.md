# References: Evaluating Limits

1. [Limit of a function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limit_of_a_function) - Wikipedia - Covers algebraic techniques for evaluating limits including factoring, rationalization, and standard limit results used throughout this chapter.

2. [Squeeze theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Squeeze_theorem) - Wikipedia - Rigorous statement and proof of the squeeze theorem with visual illustrations. Directly supports the squeeze theorem technique taught in this chapter.

3. [Indeterminate form](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indeterminate_form) - Wikipedia - Classifies the seven indeterminate forms and explains why direct substitution fails in each case. Essential background for the limit evaluation strategies in this chapter.

4. Calculus: Early Transcendentals (9th Edition) - James Stewart - Cengage Learning - Sections 2.3-2.5 cover limit laws, the squeeze theorem, and algebraic evaluation techniques with extensive worked examples.

5. Calculus (11th Edition) - Ron Larson, Bruce Edwards - Cengage Learning - Chapter 1.3 provides a systematic approach to evaluating limits algebraically with clear decision trees for choosing techniques.

6. [Evaluating Limits](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/ComputingLimits.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Detailed walkthrough of direct substitution, factoring, rationalizing, and combining fractions with step-by-step solutions.

7. [Limit Laws and Techniques](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-calculus-ab/ab-limits-new/ab-1-5a) - Khan Academy - Practice problems on applying limit properties and algebraic manipulation, with immediate feedback aligned to AP curriculum.

8. [Special Trigonometric Limits](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/LimitsProperties.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Covers the fundamental trig limits sin(x)/x and (1-cos(x))/x with geometric proofs and applications.

9. [The Squeeze Theorem](https://www.mathsisfun.com/calculus/limits-squeeze-theorem.html) - Math is Fun - Accessible visual explanation of the squeeze theorem with interactive examples that complement the chapter's MicroSim.

10. [Limit Evaluation Techniques](https://www.whitman.edu/mathematics/calculus_online/section02.03.html) - Whitman College Calculus - Open-source textbook section with clean explanations of limit evaluation methods and self-check exercises.
