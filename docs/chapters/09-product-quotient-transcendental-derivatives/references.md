# References: Product, Quotient, and Transcendental Derivatives

1. [Product rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Product_rule) - Wikipedia - Proof, geometric interpretation, and extensions of the product rule. Provides the theoretical foundation for the first major rule introduced in this chapter.

2. [Quotient rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quotient_rule) - Wikipedia - Derivation from the product and chain rules, with examples and common student pitfalls. Complements the chapter's treatment of rational function differentiation.

3. [Trigonometric functions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trigonometric_functions#Derivatives) - Wikipedia - Derivatives of all six trig functions with proofs from the limit definition. Essential reference for the transcendental derivative formulas in this chapter.

4. Calculus: Early Transcendentals (9th Edition) - James Stewart - Cengage Learning - Sections 3.2-3.3 cover the product and quotient rules, then derivatives of trig, exponential, and logarithmic functions with rich applications.

5. Thomas' Calculus (15th Edition) - Joel Hass, Christopher Heil, Maurice Weir - Pearson - Sections 3.5-3.7 provide systematic coverage of transcendental derivatives with helpful derivative tables and mnemonics.

6. [Product and Quotient Rule](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/ProductQuotientRule.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Clear worked examples showing when to use each rule, with guidance on simplification and common errors.

7. [Derivatives of Trig Functions](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-calculus-ab/ab-differentiation-1-new/ab-2-7) - Khan Academy - Interactive practice on derivatives of sin, cos, tan, and their reciprocals with AP-aligned problems.

8. [Derivatives of Exponential and Logarithmic Functions](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/DiffExpLogFcns.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Covers derivatives of e^x, a^x, ln(x), and log_a(x) with proofs and applications.

9. [Product Rule Visualization](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YG15m2VwSjA) - 3Blue1Brown - Geometric animation showing why the product rule works using area interpretation, building visual intuition beyond formulas.

10. [Derivatives of Trig Functions](https://www.mathsisfun.com/calculus/derivatives-trig.html) - Math is Fun - Quick reference with visual proofs of the six trig derivative formulas, complementing the chapter's Trig Derivative Cycle MicroSim.
