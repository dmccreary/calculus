# References: Implicit Differentiation

1. [Implicit function](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicit_function) - Wikipedia - Defines implicit vs. explicit functions and introduces the implicit function theorem. Provides theoretical context for why implicit differentiation is needed.

2. [Implicit function theorem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicit_function_theorem) - Wikipedia - Rigorous conditions under which implicit curves can be locally expressed as functions. Deeper mathematical foundation for the techniques in this chapter.

3. [Implicit curve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Implicit_curve) - Wikipedia - Gallery of famous implicit curves including circles, ellipses, and the folium of Descartes. Directly supports the curve examples used in this chapter's MicroSims.

4. Calculus: Early Transcendentals (9th Edition) - James Stewart - Cengage Learning - Section 3.5 develops implicit differentiation through worked examples of increasing complexity, including tangent lines to implicit curves.

5. Thomas' Calculus (15th Edition) - Joel Hass, Christopher Heil, Maurice Weir - Pearson - Section 3.7 covers implicit differentiation with applications to related rates and tangent lines to conic sections.

6. [Implicit Differentiation](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/ImplicitDiff.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Step-by-step examples showing the mechanics of implicit differentiation with clear treatment of the dy/dx chain rule application.

7. [Implicit Differentiation](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-calculus-ab/ab-differentiation-2-new/ab-3-2) - Khan Academy - Interactive practice on implicit differentiation including finding tangent lines, with video explanations aligned to AP curriculum.

8. [Implicit Differentiation Explained](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qb40J4N1fa4) - Organic Chemistry Tutor - Comprehensive video tutorial with examples covering circles, ellipses, and more complex implicit equations.

9. [Implicit Curves Grapher](https://www.desmos.com/calculator) - Desmos - Free online graphing tool supporting implicit equations, useful for visualizing the curves discussed in this chapter.

10. [Logarithmic Differentiation](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/LogDiff.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Covers the logarithmic differentiation technique as a special application of implicit differentiation for products and variable exponents.
