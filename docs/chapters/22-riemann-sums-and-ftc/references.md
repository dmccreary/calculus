# References: Riemann Sums and the Fundamental Theorem

1. [Riemann sum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riemann_sum) - Wikipedia - Left, right, midpoint, and trapezoidal Riemann sums with convergence to the definite integral. Core reference for the approximation methods in this chapter.

2. [Fundamental theorem of calculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fundamental_theorem_of_calculus) - Wikipedia - Both parts of the FTC with proofs and geometric interpretations. The central theorem connecting differentiation and integration presented in this chapter.

3. [Riemann integral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riemann_integral) - Wikipedia - Formal definition of the definite integral as a limit of Riemann sums. Provides the rigorous foundation for the area-under-curve concept developed in this chapter.

4. Calculus: Early Transcendentals (9th Edition) - James Stewart - Cengage Learning - Sections 5.1-5.3 develop Riemann sums, the definite integral, and both parts of the Fundamental Theorem with clear geometric motivation.

5. Thomas' Calculus (15th Edition) - Joel Hass, Christopher Heil, Maurice Weir - Pearson - Sections 5.1-5.4 provide excellent visualizations of Riemann sums converging to the integral and careful treatment of both FTC parts.

6. [Riemann Sums](https://tutorial.math.lamar.edu/Classes/CalcI/AreaProblem.aspx) - Paul's Online Math Notes - Step-by-step construction of Riemann sums with worked examples showing convergence as the number of rectangles increases.

7. [Fundamental Theorem of Calculus](https://www.khanacademy.org/math/ap-calculus-ab/ab-integration-new/ab-6-4) - Khan Academy - Interactive lessons on both FTC parts with practice evaluating definite integrals and finding derivatives of accumulation functions.

8. [Essence of Calculus: Integration](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rfG8ce4nNh0) - 3Blue1Brown - Visually stunning explanation of how area accumulation connects to antiderivatives, making the FTC feel intuitive rather than magical.

9. [Riemann Sums Visualization](https://www.desmos.com/calculator/tgyr42ezjq) - Desmos - Interactive Riemann sum visualizer where students can adjust the number of rectangles and see convergence to the definite integral.

10. [The Fundamental Theorem of Calculus](https://www.whitman.edu/mathematics/calculus_online/section07.02.html) - Whitman College Calculus - Open-source section with clear proofs of both FTC parts and exercises connecting area functions to antiderivatives.
